Test Matches

Test cricket is the form of the sport of cricket with the longest match duration and is considered the game's highest standard.

  • The first officially recognised Test match took place between 15 and 19 March 1877 between England and Australia at the Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG).
  • Two teams of 11 players each play a four-innings match, which may last up to five days.
  • It is called Test because the long, gruelling matches are mentally and physically testing.
